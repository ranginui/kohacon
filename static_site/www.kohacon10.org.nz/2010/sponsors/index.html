Sponsors

We want to thank all sponsors for their generous contributions to KohaCon. Thanks to their sponsorship, the conference can be offered for free. Our sponsors are (in alphabetical order).
BibLibre provides a range of services to libraries: we develop, host, support open source software for libraries and may also help you with migration processes, staff training and software maintenance.

Contact: Paul Poulain
Butte Public Library is Montana's first open source public library.

Contact: Lee Phillips
ByWater Solution's mission is to provide the highest quality of service for those seeking a significantly less expensive alternative to high priced software and technology solutions by utilizing the endless resources found in the open-source software community.

Contact: Brendan Gallagher
CaféNET is a public WiFi network that gives you access to the Internet while you're out and about. At KohaCon10 they allow participants to share their conference experience with other Koha users who cannot come to Wellington.

Calyx is a pioneer in end-to-end library management solutions, leveraging leading open-source technologies, best-practice and industry standards.

Contact: Irma Birchall
Catalyst IT is a New Zealand specialist in open source technologies and offers end-to-end services from consulting, software development, hosting, technical consulting to outsourced support.

Contact: Don Christie
Equinox Software unites the designers and developers of open source ILS software, advancing the future of library technology.

Contact: Galen Charlton
Graphic Press offers design, prepress, printing and binding services not only locally to Levin but nationwide.

Contact: Graphic Press
Hashigo Zake is a new beer bar in Wellington that specialises in craft beer from around the world.

Contact: Hashigo Zake
Horowhenua District Council is the government body of the Horowhenua distric about 1 hour north of Wellington where Koha was founded.

KohaAloha is a koha-community.org services company based in New Zealand.

Horowhenua Library Trust with its main library branch in Levin was the initiator of the development of Koha.

Contact: Joann Ransom
Libriotech is a company that provides services (installation, maintenance and hosting) related to Free and Open Source Software for libraries.

Contact: Magnus Enger
The Northeast Kansas Library System (NEKLS) is dedicated to providing universal access to exceptional library service through its 120 member libraries in the 14 counties of Northeast Kansas.

Contact: Sharon Moreland
RJ's Licorice is a New Zealand institution: family-owned and hands-on production of licorice with a Kiwi twist.
